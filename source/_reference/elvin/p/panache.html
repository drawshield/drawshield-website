Panache

Panache. An upright plume of more than three rows of featliers, generally of a Cock or Swan, was not unfrequent, particularly at the period shortly before th.e assumption of more distinctive crests. P. 43, f. 41. Crest of Mortimer. The Panache of Peacocks' feathers is the Crest of Sir Edmund de Thorpe, 1418. P. 43, f. 42. A Panache of Turkeys feathers the Crest of Harsicke, of Southacre, Co. Norfolk.
