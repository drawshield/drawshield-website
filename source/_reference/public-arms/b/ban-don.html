BAN DON (Co. Cork). Has no armorial bearings recorded in Ulster's Office. Upon a sheet of Irish Arms published by Messrs Marcus Ward & Company, Ltd., it is credited with the following (taken from the seal), namely, azure, over water in base proper, a bridge of seven arches, thereon at either end an embattled gateway domed, argent, in the centre chief point an escutcheon parted per bend embattled of the last and gules, surmounted by an Earl's coronet proper. (The arms of Boyle, Earls of Cork and Orrery.)
