BANGOR (Carnarvonshire). Has no armorial bearings. Burke's " General Armory " quotes the arms as the same as those of the See of Bangor, which are " Gules, a bend or, guttee-de-poix between two mullets pierced argent." The seal of the Corporation, however, has an escutcheon gules, on a bend or, guttee-de-poix, a bend wavy azure, thereon a representation of a mace . . ., all between two mullets argent.
