GILLINGHAM, Borough of (Kent). Argent, a cross gules, in the first quarter an ancient harp, in the second, on waves of the sea an ancient ship, in the third issuing out of waves of the sea a rock, thereon a fort, and in the fourth quarter a sprig of broom, all proper. Crest — On a wreath of the colours, in front of a fouled anchor erect, two swords in saltire points upwards, that pointing to the dexter sheathed all proper. Motto — " With fort and fleet for home and England."

[Granted, College of Arms, April 22, 1904.]
