GODALMING (Surrey). Party per pale gules and sable, a woolpack argent, on a chief of the last, a rose of the first, barbed and seeded proper, between two escocheons also gules, that on the dexter charged with a fesse dancettee between two crosses pattee in palt of the third, and that on the sinister charged with three pears in bend leaved and slipped proper. Cj-est — On a wreath of the colours, a mound, thereon a ram statant holding in the mouth a pear leaved and slipped all proper, suspended from the neck by a riband gules an escocheon or, charged with a pair of shears erect points upwards, also proper. Motto — " Libera deinde fidelis."

[Granted, College of Arms, 12th June 1893.]
