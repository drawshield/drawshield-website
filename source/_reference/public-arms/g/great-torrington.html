GREAT TORRINGTON (Devonshire). Argent, in base two bars wavy, over all a fleur-de-lis within a bordure engrailed, all sable. Confirmed by Harvey, Clarenceux, 6th September 1564, and also recorded at the Visitation of Devonshire, 1620.

Berry makes the base barry wavy of six argent and azure, and does not engrail the bordure. The Corporation notepaper shows the fleur-de-lis in chief and not over all.
