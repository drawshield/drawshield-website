NASSAU, See of (West Indies). Argent, a landscape, in base on a rock, an open Bible at the foot of an lona cross : behind it the open sea, thereon a ship sailing to the sinister and a palm-covered land. [E.x. Woodward.] Gules, an lona cross proper, on a chief dancette or, on a pale azure between two palm trees proper, a ship.

[Both of no authority.]
