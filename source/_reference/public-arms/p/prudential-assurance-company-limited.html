PRUDENTIAL ASSURANCE COMPANY, LIMITED (London). Sable, three bars embattled or, within two flaunches argent, each charged with three martlets in pale gules. Crest — A female figure proper, vested argent, cloaked and girdled gules, resting the sinister arm on the trunk of an oak-tree eradicated and sprouting, thereon an hour-glass, and holding in the dexter hand a mirror which she is regarding, and in the sinister an arrow entwined by a serpent all proper. Motto — " Fortis qui prudens."

[Granted, College of Arms, March 15, 1904.]
