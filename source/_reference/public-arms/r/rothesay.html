ROTHESAY (Buteshire). Has not matriculated any armorial bearings. Those in use are party per pale, the dexter side argent, a castle triple-towered between in chief, on the dexter a crescent and on the sinister a mullet, and in base a lymphad, sail furled, the sinister side being the arms of Stewart or, a fesse chequy azure and argent. The seal represents the foregoing arms, within the legend, " Libertas, datur, Villa; de Rothisea per Robertum Stuart, Rcgem Scottor."
