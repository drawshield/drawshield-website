   [Arms of Robert Devereux, 2nd Earl of Essex] 
Quarterly
    1st arg a fess gu in chief 3 torteaux (Devereux),  
    2nd arg a cross engrailed gu between 4 water bougets sa much larger (Bourchier), 
    3rd quarterly 
        1st and 4th (France Moderne) az 3 fleurs de lys or 
        2nd and 3rd (England) gu 3 lions passant guardant in pale or armed and langued az;; 
        all within a bordure arg (Thomas of Woodstock),   
    4th az a bend arg cotised or between 6 lions rampant or larger (Bohun),  
    5th gu (2 bendlets the upper or the lower arg) a bendlet or enhanced 
        a bendlet arg abased  (Roger FitzMiles, Earl of Hereford),  
    6th quarterly or and gu (overall) a carbuncle sa larger (Mandeville),  
    7th gu billetty or a fess arg (Louvain), 
    8th arg a fess gu a canton and a canton abased gu (Wydville),  
    9th arg a saltire fretty gu and or (Crophull), 
    10th or a fret gu (Verdon/Verdun),  
    11th per pale or and vert a lion rampant gu lower larger 
        (Marshal, Earl of Pembroke, as Earl Marshal),
    12th gu a bend (fusilly) lozengy or (Marshal, ancient),  
    13th vairy or and gu (Ferrers),                     
    14th az 3 garbs or larger (Blondeville, Earl of Chester),                     
    15th gu 7 lozenges voided conjoined 3,3 and 1 or (Quincy),  
    16th gu a cinquefoil arg (Beaumont)       			
        smaller much higher to the dexter to the dexter
